Bettiah is a city and administrative headquarters of West Champaran district -, near Indo-Nepal border, 225 kilometres north-west of Patna, in Bihar state of India.


Bettiah – City of Princely Heritage

Stepping into Bettiah, you are transported back in time through the numerous historical sites and monuments that adorn the cityscape. During the Mughal era, it was the stronghold of the formidable zamindari estate known as Bettiah Raj. Situated in Bihar’s West Champaran region, Bettiah is also renowned for its significant role in India’s freedom struggle. It was at the heart of Mahatma Gandhi’s Champaran Satyagraha movement. Once teeming with vast indigo plantations, Bettiah is today home to small farms of wheat and sugarcane that add to its rustic appeal. Located close to the Indo-Nepal border, Bettiah’s vibrant cultural legacy makes it a treat for travelers.

Unraveling Bettiah’s Delights
Bettiah is strewn with marvelous reminders of its past splendor. To get a closer look at the city’s zamindari legacy, visit the Raj Kachahri. The magnificent Circuit House takes you back to the colonial era. Bettiah is also worth visiting for its religious significance. Some of the prominent Hindu shrines here include the Durga Mandir, Kaali Bagh Mandir, and Santwan Das Math. A 17th century Catholic Missionary Church is also worth visiting. A great time to visit the city is between the festivals of Dussehra and Diwali when an annual fair is held at the Raj Kachahri courtyard.

Moving Around
There is no dearth of efficient and accessible local transport in Bettiah. City buses ply on major routes within the city and also connect it to suburban regions. However, cycle rickshaws are the most common means of transport in Bettiah. They can be hired directly from the streets at affordable fares. Auto-rickshaws are also available in most parts of the city.

Friendly Tips
Bettiah experiences severe heat during summer making it an unfavorable season for travelers. As Bettiah is a small town, most areas are isolated at night and it is advisable to stay indoors.
